How much do you know about Malaysian food?


Explore the diverse cuisines of Malaysia:

Malaysian cuisine, Malaysian cuisine or Malaysian cuisine (Malay: Masakan Malaysia), is a local cuisine
in Malaysia, famous for its unique and diverse flavor. It mainly evolved from the unique cuisine of
various ethnic groups such as the Malay aboriginal tribes, Chinese immigrants, Indian immigrants,
Indonesian immigrants, etc. It is greatly influenced by the ingredients and cooking methods of other
ethnic groups, reflecting the multi-ethnic culture of Malaysia. In the process of mutual exchange, the
elements gradually evolved into various Malay dishes influenced by dishes from various places.
        
Malaysian cuisine uses rice or noodles as the staple food, and usually uses meat or fish cooked in
different flavors as the main course, accompanied by steamed vegetables. The main course food is mostly
eaten with sauce. Generally speaking, Malaysians like to eat spicy food. The spiciness is mainly from
the spiciness in chili peppers, which is spicier than in Thailand.
        
Although Malaysia is a Muslim-majority country with 60% of the population being Muslims, the government
allows non-halal foods such as pork, crabs and alcoholic beverages to be sold in major supermarkets or
wet markets.




Indian Food

Roti Canai

The Malay word for Indian pancake is roti canai. Roti refers to "bread", here it means "pancake", and canai means "flattening back and forth", just because the Indian pancake master flattens, air-dries and stretches the dough in the process of making Indian pancakes. There is also a legend that the origin of canai comes from the dough (channal) used when making pancakes.

Another theory is that the word canai comes from the city of Chennai in India, because when a large number of Indians migrated to Malaysia for gold mining, the Indian pancake chefs at that time were all from Chennai, India.

In India and Singapore, Indian pancakes are also called "prata" pancakes. The shapes of Indian pancakes and burrata pancakes are the same, and burrata pancakes also retain the characteristics of Indian pancakes: round, but usually the ones with fillings are square, which is said to be easier to cook.

There are many flavors in Malaysia, as follows: Roti Kosong (no filling) Roti Telur (egg flavor) Roti Pisang (banana flavor) Roti Planta (butter flavor), etc. Different regions have different flavors.

Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish originating among the Muslims of South Asia. It is made with spices, vegetables, rice, and usually some type of meat (chicken, beef, goat, lamb, prawn, or fish). In some cases without any meat, and sometimes with eggs and potatoes.

Biryani is one of the most popular dishes in South Asia, as well as among the diaspora from the region. Similar dishes are also prepared in other parts of the world such as in Iraq, Myanmar, Thailand, and Malaysia.Biryani is the single most-ordered dish on Indian online food ordering and delivery services, and has been labelled as the most popular dish overall in India.

Tandoori Chicken

This Indian Tandoor Grilled Chicken (Tanddoori Chicken) is a grilled chicken that most friends who have eaten Indian food will have tasted. It is marinated in spices and yogurt and grilled in an upright circular oven.

The same oven is also used to bake Indian naan. The firepower is very strong, and the baked food is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, which is very delicious. Even if you don’t have a Tandoor oven at home, you can still make delicious Tandoori chicken in the oven!

The traditional Tandoori grilled chicken is red. In ancient times, it was dyed with saffron. Nowadays, most restaurants use pigments instead of expensive saffron.

Malay Food

Nasi Lemak

The Malay word for nasi lemak is "nasi lemak", "nasi" means rice, and lemak originally means fat, which here refers to coconut milk; it is named after soaking rice in coconut milk and then cooking it into rice. Generally, an ordinary pot or rice cooker can be used; sometimes knotted pandan leaves are put into the pot and cooked together. Spices such as turmeric and lemongrass can also be added to increase the aroma of nasi lemak.

A pack of nasi lemak is served on a plate with banana leaves as the base, sliced cucumbers, small anchovies, roasted peanuts, and some are served with fried kangkong and hard-boiled eggs (boiled or fried), Indian pickles and sambal (a sauce made from shrimp paste and chilli).

Many nasi lemak rice dishes also come with other ingredients such as chicken, octopus or squid, clams, biscotti, beef curry (beef simmered in a mixture of coconut milk and spices) or balu (beef lungs). Traditionally, most condiments are hot and spicy, as well as the cooling and antipyretic cucumber.

Traditional nasi lemak is eaten for breakfast in the morning. You can see it being sold at roadside stalls in Malaysia starting in the early morning. Now due to the popularity of nasi lemak, it is basically sold at market stalls or stores. It is usually sold wrapped in newspapers, oil paper or banana leaves. Regardless, some restaurants serve it on a plate for lunch or dinner.

Rendang

Rendang (also known as Padang in Hong Kong) is a spicy meat delicacy that originated from the Minangkabau people in Indonesia. As one of the representatives of Minangkabau food culture, it often becomes a delicacy on the table in formal occasions or when showing respect to guests.

Rendang is also common among the Malays of Malaysia, Singapore and Brunei, and among the Moros in the southern Philippines. Traditionally, the Minangkabau people make rendang during festive occasions, such as traditional ceremonies, weddings, and Hari Raya Aidilfitri.

Although rendang is often considered a type of curry, it is not typically classified as such in Indonesia because rendang is richer and contains less liquid than the average Indonesian curry.

Asam Pedas

Asam pedas is a Maritime Southeast Asian sour and spicy fish stew dish. Asam pedas is believed to come from Minangkabau cuisine of West Sumatra, Indonesia and has spread throughout to the islands of Sumatra (inc. Riau, Jambi, South Sumatra), Borneo (West Kalimantan) and Malay Peninsula.

Assam Pedas is a classic Malaysian dish that's sour, fiery hot, and tastes extraordinarily satisfying. Everyone has their own interpretation for this favorite dish and there are endless adaptations; suffice it to say, it’s sour, fiery hot, and tastes extraordinarily satisfying.

Chinese Food

Yue Bing

"Ji Yue" is a very ancient custom in China. It is actually an activity of worship of the "Moon God" by the ancients. Eating moon cakes and admiring the moon during the Mid-Autumn Festival are indispensable customs in the Mid-Autumn Festival in northern and southern China. Moon cakes symbolize reunion, and people regard them as festive food, using them to worship the moon and give them to relatives and friends.

"Ji Yue" is a very ancient custom in China. Moon cakes are an offering to worship the moon god during the Mid-Autumn Festival in ancient times, and they are also a seasonal food during the Mid-Autumn Festival. In ancient times, moon worship was held every Mid-Autumn Festival night. Set up a large incense table and place mooncakes, fruits and other offerings. Under the moon, the moon statue is placed in the direction of the moon, with red candles burning high. The whole family worships the moon in turn, and then the housewife cuts the reunion moon cakes.

Hotpot

Hot pot is one of China's original delicacies and is a food suitable for all ages. Hot pot is not only a way of cooking, but also a symbol of culture.

Hot pot generally refers to a cooking method that uses a pot as an utensil, uses a heat source to boil the pot, and boils water or soup to cook various foods. It can also refer to the pots used in this cooking method. Its characteristic is that you can eat while cooking, or the pot itself has a heat preservation effect, so the food is still steaming hot when you eat, and the soup is integrated into one.

Typical hot pot ingredients include various meats, seafood, vegetables, soy products, mushrooms, egg products, staple foods, etc., which are boiled in boiling water or a special stock pot and then eaten. Some ways of eating it include dipping it in seasonings and eating it together.

Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao chicken with a spicy lychee flavor, is a Sichuan-style dish that originated from Guizhou cuisine, spread to Shandong cuisine, and then became Sichuan cuisine. Guizhou, Shandong and Sichuan do not cook this dish exactly the same way, and the names are also different. Guizhou calls it spicy chicken, Shandong calls it stir-fried chicken, and Sichuan calls it kung pao chicken.

Kung Pao Chicken is made of chicken as the main ingredient, with peanuts, peppers and other auxiliary ingredients; it is red but not spicy, hot but not strong, spicy and spicy, and the meat is smooth and crispy; its entrance is spicy and the chicken is tender Can be paired with the crunchiness of peanuts.
